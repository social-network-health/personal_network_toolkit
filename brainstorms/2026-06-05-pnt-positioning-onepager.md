# Personal Network Toolkit — Positioning One-Pager
*Derived from the 2026-06-05 grill session. Working copy; raw capture lives in `2026-06-05-pnt-positioning.md`.*

## Mission (the headline)
**Improve the health of your personal networks — the communities with you at the center.**

## What PNT is
The **open recipe** for building local-first apps that turn your contacts into **private relationship memory you own** — a spec + reference designs + MCP servers + lint + an agent skill. An AI agent builds a conformant app, or validates one it didn't build, against typed, checkable contracts.

> **PNT doesn't ship the app. It makes the app prove it protects your data.**

*(A home-cooked meal — and we open-source the recipe.)*

## Why it's the right problem
- **Leverage.** The personal-network root is the highest-leverage, *most underserved* place in local-first. The field is crowded into sync and CRDTs; almost no one works on **comfort with — and access to — your own relationship data.**
- **Stakes.** It's a **community-mental-health** problem, not just a privacy one.
- **Threat model.** The data you reach for in a crisis — who to call when you've got nothing left, which family member to apologize to — is exactly what's unreasonable to hand a profit-motivated SaaS with a swappable board. So today it lives nowhere, or only in memory, and you run out of time and energy before you reach the right person.

## Two doors, one spine

**Door A — the cause (talks, demo table, dweb):**
> The root of your personal network — your people and the private memory that decorates them — is the highest-leverage, most underserved place in local-first, and it's a community-mental-health problem, not just a privacy one.
>
> If you believe people should be able to **keep and use** the relationships they rely on — for life, out of reach of anyone who'd sell or lock them — let's build the tools that make it safe. **If you don't, come break the thesis.**

*Supporting — the quartet:*
> Solid asks where personal data can live. AT Protocol asks how social records interoperate. JSContact asks how contact cards should be represented. **PNAs ask what guarantees an app must make when it turns contact data into private relationship memory.**

**Door B — the builder (README, cold read):**
> Personal Network Toolkit is a spec + reference designs + MCP servers + lint + an agent skill for building local-first apps that turn contact data into private relationship memory the user owns. An agent builds a conformant app — or validates one it didn't build — against typed, checkable contracts. It doesn't ship the app; it makes the app prove it protects your data.

## The timely hook (AI-OS era)
The AI-OS is coming either way. The only question is whether your most intimate relationship memory gets absorbed into someone else's **cloud** AI-OS, or lives in a **sovereign local layer the AI operates on under your consent.** PNT is the spec for the second one.

## Say this, not that
- Say **"private relationship memory."** Not "a data store."
- Lead with the **relationships** (and *using* them) — not "the local root" jargon, not "privacy."
- **"Data store plus protections"** is a *mechanism clause*, not a headline. Demote it.
